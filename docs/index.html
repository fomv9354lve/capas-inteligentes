Claim Drift Example

CAPAS is built for the moment where a sourced scientific statement becomes a reusable data record. That is where wording drift creates downstream risk.

Paper evidenceAssociation observed

The source reports a directional association under limited conditions. It does not establish intervention, temporal order, or confounder control.

Dataset claim driftCausal improvement stated

The candidate training record says the treatment causes improvement, turning a limited finding into a stronger reusable claim.

CAPAS decisionREWRITE

Evidence supports association. It does not license causality. Rewrite the claim boundary before reuse.

Why claim admissibility?

Datasets govern records. Provenance systems govern sources. CAPAS governs the claim itself: the exact reusable sentence and whether the supplied evidence licenses it.

Record-level tools miss wording drift

A dataset can have a source, license, and lineage while still containing a claim that says more than the source supports.

Fact-checkers answer a different question

CAPAS does not ask whether the world is true. It asks whether this evidence packet licenses this claim boundary.

Governance needs a decision artifact

ACCEPT, REWRITE, REJECT, and HOLD create an exception queue, not just another annotation or confidence score.

Why not just use human review?

CAPAS does not replace expert review. It turns expert review into a reusable decision system: explicit criteria, repeatable boundaries, and exportable exceptions.

Human review alone

  • Criteria live in reviewer memory, SOP prose, or spreadsheet comments.
  • Wording decisions vary by reviewer, queue pressure, and local precedent.
  • Exceptions are hard to reuse across batches, teams, and audits.
  • Review creates judgment, but not always a structured decision artifact.

CAPAS + reviewers

  • Evidence contracts make admissibility criteria explicit by claim type.
  • ACCEPT, REWRITE, REJECT, and HOLD are deterministic and repeatable.
  • Every exception becomes a queue item with blockers and rationale.
  • Review produces an exportable audit artifact, not only a one-off opinion.

The operating loop

CAPAS ends when the claim boundary is resolved. A REWRITE is not a dead end; it is the handoff into correction, resubmission, ACCEPT, and governed reuse.

1Candidate claim

Evidence and wording enter as a reusable record candidate.

2CAPAS gate

The deterministic engine checks the evidence contract.

3REWRITE

The claim boundary is too strong for the supplied evidence.

4Correction

The reviewer narrows the sentence to the licensed scope.

5Resubmit

The corrected record runs through the same deterministic gate again.

6ACCEPT + audit trail

The accepted claim enters the dataset with its decision history attached.

Audit artifact

CAPAS does not just label a record. It produces a decision object that reviewers can inspect, export, and challenge.

Modeling notes

  1. Stress-test counts refer to structured CAPAS payload and UI/CLI validation runs, not a public customer production deployment.
  2. Review-capacity estimates are planning assumptions. They are not booked savings and must be calibrated against an organization's actual review baseline.
  3. CAPAS gates supplied evidence fields. It does not infer hidden evidence, provide legal advice, certify broad scientific truth, or replace external review.
  4. Customer documents, schemas, and provenance packets may contain proprietary or licensed material. Do not share payload URLs or exports containing sensitive claim text, reviewer identifiers, source paths, or third-party licensed content without permission.
