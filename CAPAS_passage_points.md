## 2. The TWO strongest folds

### **POINT OF PASSAGE A — Pharma CDS result attestation (P1)**
### **POINT OF PASSAGE B — Clinical-trial database-lock-to-submission (P3)**

**Why these two beat the rest — the decisive cuts:**

1. **Determinism is real, not aspirational.** The single most disqualifying flaw in P4/P5/P7/P8 is that modern ML/LLM outputs are *stochastic by construction* (temperature > 0), which the findings themselves flag as the SR 11-7 "breakage point." A re-derivation gate's whole value is bit-exactness; the moment the underlying result isn't deterministic, CAPAS degrades to "within a declared tolerance," which is exactly the soft ground incumbents already occupy. **P1 (peak integration: raw data + fixed integration parameters → number) and P3 (SDTM + pinned SAS/R → ADaM/TLF) are genuinely deterministic computations.** This is the cleanest re-derivation fit in the entire landscape, and it is not a coincidence that the regulator (Annex 22) wrote the spec *as* determinism.

2. **The demand is verbatim re-derivation, with money and a date.** P1: Annex 22's "static deterministic model — same input always yields same result," final mid-2026, against 60–80%-of-warning-letters exposure and $100M–$1B consent-decree remediation. P3: CDISC ARM exists *literally* so reviewers can "reproduce analysis results," plus FDAAA 801's $10k/day, plus the 74% discrepancy rate that *quantifies* the unmet demand. These aren't "reasonable-care benchmarks" (NIST, P8) or deferred obligations (P5/P7's 2 Dec 2027 slip) — they're dated, fined, and the budget *already exists* (P21 licensing, manual reviewer reproduction, consent-decree spend).

3. **The incumbent gap is structural and total — and the incumbents have publicly conceded the exact boundary.** Empower tells reviewers to "consider only the highest-numbered result-set ID" and treats "excessive reprocessing as a negative indicator" — i.e., it manages the *trust heuristic* instead of re-deriving. Pinnacle 21 checks *conformance*, not numbers ("does the file conform" vs "does the number reproduce"). These vendors have drawn their own product boundary *short of* re-derivation. That's a cleaner gap than P6, where Code Ocean is one feature away ("runs cleanly" → "regenerates the paper's numbers") and could plausibly extend.

4. **One receipt clears multiple binding demands at once (leverage).** P3 is uniquely efficient: a single re-derivation event satisfies ALCOA+/E6(R3) reconstruction + ARM "reproduce results" + TRC conformance + registry reconciliation, because *the same re-derived numbers feed the table, the Define-XML, and the registry posting*. P1 similarly clears Annex 22 + ALCOA+ "Accurate = verified" + the four-eyes review + the inspector demand in one artifact. Multi-demand leverage per receipt is the highest in pharma/clinical.

**Why not P4 (the runner-up):** It has the best *verbatim* mandate (SS1/23 "replicate any parameter estimation and model results") but two structural problems P1/P3 don't have: (i) the high-value targets are increasingly *stochastic AI/ML and proprietary vendor models* — where determinism is contested and CAPAS can't always pin the pipeline without source access; (ii) the deterministic, classical-model slice where CAPAS *would* shine is precisely where validators already do manual replication and the pain is most tolerated. P3 gives you the same "independent replication" demand on a *fully deterministic* artifact (SAS/R on locked SDTM) with a harder deadline and a quantified failure rate. P4 is the third fold to build, not the first two.

## 3. The two folds, concretely

### POINT OF PASSAGE A — Pharma CDS / GxP result attestation

- **Exact wedge:** The result-attestation / second-person ("four-eyes") QC review step, where a chromatographic assay/purity result is signed off and the reviewer + inspector must currently *trust* that "raw data + declared integration method = reported number." Today that bridge is crossed the long way (manual audit-trail re-read, reprocessing-count heuristics, "highest result-set ID" convention). CAPAS replaces the human audit-trail re-read with a one-line deterministic pass/fail.
- **Artifact CAPAS ingests:** The CDS raw-data export (Empower result set / Chromeleon sequence) + the declared processing method/integration parameters + any Excel/macro calculation sheet used for assay/stability math.
- **What CAPAS does:** Independently re-executes the integration/calibration/calculation and emits a receipt `{raw-data hash, method/parameter hash, re-derived value, reported value, match verdict, timestamp, signer}`.
- **Where the receipt attaches:** As a controlled object in the eQMS/VLMS of record (bound to the Veeva/ValGenesis/Kneat release record) and appended to the CDS result-set and the QC review package, so it travels with the audit-trail review. Verification layer *beneath* the system of record, not a replacement.
- **First buyer:** QC/QA at a site under active data-integrity pressure — concretely, a **CDMO or an Indian-site manufacturer** (~60% DI-warning-letter rate vs ~10% US) facing a warning letter, import alert, or a consent decree with third-party oversight. The consent-decree population is the sharpest first buyer: third-party oversight is *mandated and budgeted*, and a portable re-derivation receipt is exactly what the overseer and FDA want. Second buyer: any QC org racing the mid-2026 Annex 22 deadline.
- **Why the incumbent cannot just add it (structural reason):** Empower and Chromeleon are *the system that produced the number*. For a re-derivation to be evidence, it must come from an **independent engine** — a re-run inside the same CDS proves nothing an inspector will credit, because the thing under suspicion is the CDS's own processing. The vendor literally cannot self-attest its own determinism in a way that satisfies "independent third party can re-check." Worse, building a true second engine cannibalizes their own audit-trail-and-heuristics compliance story ("highest result-set ID," "excessive reprocessing is bad") that they've sold for a decade. CAPAS's *vendor-neutrality is the product*; the CDS's *vendor-identity is the disqualification*.

### POINT OF PASSAGE B — Clinical-trial database-lock → submission

- **Exact wedge:** The database-lock-to-submission moment, where locked SDTM + analysis programs + Define-XML/ARM should be re-executed in a clean environment to confirm the submitted ADaM datasets, result tables, and registry figures are the bit-exact deterministic output of the source data. Today bridged the long way: sponsor attests → Pinnacle 21 checks format only → FDA reviewer *maybe* re-runs manually → registry entry typed separately.
- **Artifact CAPAS ingests:** The locked study data package exported from Rave/Oracle/Vault at DB lock — SDTM (+ DM/ADSL), the SAS/R analysis programs, Define-XML with ARM, and the result shells/TLFs.
- **What CAPAS does:** Re-executes the derivation deterministically (pinned code + environment) and emits a receipt: hashes of inputs/programs/runtime + pass/fail diff of re-derived vs submitted ADaM and tables + a **registry-figure reconciliation block** (ClinicalTrials.gov/EudraCT vs re-derived).
- **Where the receipt attaches:** (a) into the eCTD/Define-XML package as a re-checkable companion to the Study Data Reviewer's Guide; (b) into the eTMF as the integrity record for the DB-lock milestone; (c) alongside the registry results submission as the reconciliation proof. Plugs in *next to Pinnacle 21* — "P21 = does the file conform; CAPAS = does the number reproduce."
- **First buyer:** The **sponsor/CRO biostatistics + QA function** preparing a pivotal submission, where a non-reproducible result triggers Application-Integrity-Policy-style rejection of the *entire* submission (the most expensive possible failure; cf. Raptim, Mid-Link, Vedic 2025–26). They already pay for P21 licensing, SDRG authoring, and absorb the cost/risk of the FDA reviewer's manual re-run; CAPAS substitutes a cheaper, portable, automatic receipt. Second buyer: M&A/licensing due-diligence teams re-checking a target's pivotal data without rebuilding the environment.
- **Why the incumbent cannot just add it (structural reason):** The split is architectural, not feature-level. **EDC vendors (Medidata/Oracle/Veeva) are document/capture/workflow systems with no computational engine** — re-deriving ADaM from SDTM is outside their data model entirely; it's a different class of product. **Pinnacle 21 is committed to conformance**, and the FDA itself runs the P21 build at the gateway — P21's whole regulatory standing rests on being a *format* gate; turning it into a *numerical re-derivation* engine would put it in the position of re-running and potentially contradicting the sponsor's statistical programming, a liability posture it has deliberately avoided. And the registry (ClinicalTrials.gov) is a *store*, not a *computer* — it never touches the source. No incumbent owns "re-execute SAS/R on locked SDTM and diff against the submission *and* the registry"; it sits in the seam between four products, which is exactly why the 74% discrepancy persists.

## 4. Strongest counter-argument to each — and honest resolution

### Counter to A (Pharma CDS):
**"Re-derivation requires the exact integration algorithm, and peak integration is full of vendor-proprietary, partly-manual choices (manual baseline drops, peak-skim settings, vendor-specific apex/valley detection). CAPAS can't reproduce Empower's number bit-for-bit because it can't replicate Empower's closed integration math or the analyst's manual touch-ups — so it produces false mismatches and dies in validation hell."**

**Resolution (honest):** This is the real risk and it bounds the wedge. The fix is to scope CAPAS to what is *declared and parameterized*, not to reverse-engineer the vendor kernel. Two moves: (i) Re-derive the **downstream deterministic layer first** — the calibration-curve → concentration and the Excel/macro assay/stability math sitting *on top of* the integrated areas. That layer is unambiguously deterministic, is where many 483s actually live (spreadsheet integrity), and needs no vendor kernel. (ii) For peak integration itself, target the **processing-method-driven (automatic) integration** path, where parameters fully determine the result, and treat *manual reintegration* as an explicit "human-override" branch the receipt flags rather than reproduces — which is *exactly* what inspectors want surfaced anyway ("excessive reprocessing is a negative indicator"). So CAPAS doesn't need to beat Empower's kernel; it re-derives the parameterized + spreadsheet layers and exposes the manual-override surface. The counter is real but it *narrows the entry point to the highest-value, cleanest slice* rather than killing it.

### Counter to B (Clinical-trial):
**"Sponsors will never hand a third party their locked SDTM, proprietary SAS programs, and unblinded result data — and SAS re-execution isn't reliably deterministic across versions/OS/locale/floating-point/PROC defaults, so the 'bit-exact' diff will throw spurious mismatches and sponsors will reject a tool that contradicts their own validated programming."**

**Resolution (honest):** Two parts. **On data access:** CAPAS does not need to be a cloud service that ingests unblinded data — it runs *inside the sponsor's/CRO's validated environment* (the same place the programming already lives) and emits only the *receipt* (hashes + verdict), which is what travels to the FDA/partner. The portable artifact is the proof, not the data; this is precisely the "re-check without rebuilding the sponsor's environment" design already in the findings. **On determinism:** bit-exact across arbitrary SAS versions is genuinely fragile, so the receipt must pin the **environment** (SAS version, OS, locale, seeds) as part of the content-addressed inputs and assert reproduction *within that pinned environment* — and define declared numerical tolerances for legitimate floating-point variation, with anything outside tolerance flagged. This is weaker than "byte-for-byte across all environments" but it is *exactly the reconstruction ICH E6(R3)/ARM demand* ("an independent party can reproduce *the* analysis"), and it still catches the dominant real failure modes the findings name — missing packages, bad file paths, missing objects, library-loading errors, and the 74% registry-vs-source drift. The honest concession: CAPAS sells "reproducible within a pinned, declared environment to a stated tolerance," not metaphysical bit-identity — and that is sufficient to satisfy the mandate and collapse the discrepancy rate.

---

**Bottom line:** The two folds are **(A) the pharma CDS/GxP result-attestation step** and **(B) the clinical-trial database-lock-to-submission step**. Both sit on genuinely deterministic computations, both face dated + fined + already-budgeted demands written *as* reproducibility (Annex 22; CDISC ARM / FDAAA 801), both have a structural incumbent gap (the CDS can't independently attest its own number; the EDC/P21/registry split leaves "re-execute and diff" un-owned), and both let a single portable receipt clear multiple binding demands at once. P4 (financial MRM) is the third fold to build once the determinism harness is proven; P5/P7/P8 wait on the 2027 EU cliff and the unsolved stochastic-LLM determinism problem.

# CAPAS — Passage Points: Integrating Re-derivation into the Regulatory Stack

## 1. Thesis

Every regulated domain today **meets** its integrity obligations the same three ways — it **manages** the lifecycle (inventory, workflow, change control), **documents** it (auto-generated dossiers, model cards, Define-XML, Annex IV files), and **attests** it (e-signatures, tamper-evident audit trails, C2PA manifests, ISO 42001 certificates, Big-4 opinions). All three are *assertions about a result plus a record that the assertion was made*. The structural gap is that **nothing re-derives the result itself**: no incumbent re-executes the regulated computation on the declared inputs and confirms the reported number actually falls back out. The CDS audit trail proves *who reprocessed and why*, not that the assay value is the deterministic output of the method; Pinnacle 21 proves the file *conforms*, not that the table *reproduces*; ValidMind proves a test *was run and rendered*, not that the model *re-runs to the same number*; C2PA proves *who signed*, not that the content is real. Correctness is everywhere **inferred from process evidence** rather than **demonstrated by re-execution** — even though the regulators increasingly write the demand *as re-derivation* (Annex 22's "static deterministic model — same input always yields the same result"; CDISC ARM's "reproduce analysis results"; SS1/23's "replicate any parameter estimation and model results"). The fold is to replace the long trust-chain with a single deterministic re-derivation gate emitting a portable, third-party-re-checkable receipt.

- **Counter-argument resolved** — *"Sponsors will never hand a third party unblinded locked SDTM + proprietary SAS, and SAS re-execution isn't deterministic across versions/OS/locale/floating-point, so the diff throws spurious mismatches and sponsors reject a tool that contradicts their validated programming."* Two parts. **Data access:** CAPAS is not a cloud service that ingests unblinded data — it runs **inside the sponsor's/CRO's validated environment** (where the programming already lives) and emits only the *receipt* (hashes + verdict); the portable artifact is the proof, not the data. **Determinism:** bit-exactness across arbitrary SAS versions is genuinely fragile, so the receipt **pins the environment** (SAS version, OS, locale, seeds) as content-addressed inputs and asserts reproduction *within that pinned environment*, with **declared numerical tolerances** for legitimate floating-point variation and anything outside tolerance flagged. Weaker than metaphysical byte-identity, but it is *exactly* the reconstruction ICH E6(R3)/ARM demand ("an independent party can reproduce *the* analysis"), and it still catches the dominant real failure modes — missing packages, bad paths, missing objects, library-loading errors, and the 74% registry drift. Honest concession: CAPAS sells "reproducible within a pinned, declared environment to a stated tolerance," which is sufficient to satisfy the mandate and collapse the discrepancy rate.
