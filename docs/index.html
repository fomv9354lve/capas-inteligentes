Deterministic Claim Admissibility Engine

Stop claim drift before scientific claims enter datasets or fine-tuning.

CAPAS decides whether supplied evidence licenses a scientific claim for controlled reuse. It returns ACCEPT, REWRITE, REJECT, or HOLD with provenance blockers and audit-ready reasoning.

It does not certify scientific truth. It prevents a paper's limited evidence from turning into an over-scoped training example, report sentence, or governed dataset record.

The so what

Claim contamination avoided

The cost is not only reviewer time. It is unsupported claims entering datasets, reports, and model behavior as if the evidence licensed them.

Exception queue created

Reviewers focus on HOLD, REWRITE, REJECT, and provenance exceptions instead of treating every candidate claim as equally risky.

Audit-ready evidence trail

Each accepted claim carries a decision reason, source spans, reviewer/witness provenance, and a non-LLM decision marker.

The problem is claim drift

The problem is not lack of evidence. The problem is claim drift: the final sentence often says more than the evidence licenses.

Before

Evidence review happens in spreadsheets and prose. The final claim text can drift away from what the evidence actually licenses.

CAPAS gate

Each claim gets a type, required evidence fields, schema validation, provenance blockers, and a deterministic decision.

After

Reviewers focus on HOLD/REWRITE/REJECT queues, while accepted claims carry explicit audit trails into reports and pipelines.

Why now

Scientific AI is moving faster than review workflows

Foundation-model teams, research groups, and domain-data owners are turning source material into reusable training and evaluation records.

Synthetic and curated data amplify small wording errors

A single over-scoped claim can be copied, transformed, and learned as if it were licensed fact.

Training-data scrutiny is becoming operational

Governance teams increasingly need evidence trails, decision criteria, and documented controls before reuse.

Primary buyer story

For AI governance, research operations, and scientific data teams, CAPAS answers the question: which structured evidence records are sufficiently licensed for reuse, publication workflows, or fine-tuning preparation?

The product is best understood as a deterministic claim admissibility engine: claim plus evidence goes in; a licensed decision boundary comes out.

Advisory delivery wedge

The transformation story is not "install a validator." It is "stand up a training-data assurance control in two weeks." A delivery partner can package CAPAS as a governed workflow: ingest source material, confirm evidence spans, gate records deterministically, export an audit packet, and quantify review capacity redirected to higher-risk exceptions.

Modeling notes

  1. Stress-test counts refer to structured CAPAS payload and UI/CLI validation runs, not a public customer production deployment.
  2. Review-capacity estimates are planning assumptions. They are not booked savings and must be calibrated against an organization's actual review baseline.
  3. CAPAS gates supplied evidence fields. It does not infer hidden evidence, provide legal advice, certify broad scientific truth, or replace external review.
  4. Customer documents, schemas, and provenance packets may contain proprietary or licensed material. Do not share payload URLs or exports containing sensitive claim text, reviewer identifiers, source paths, or third-party licensed content without permission.
