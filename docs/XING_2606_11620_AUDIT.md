# Xing et al. 2606.11620 Audit Against CAPAS

Source:

- Honjar Xing, Yehong Jiang, Xianbang Wang, Zehua Wang, Zhicheng Jiang,
  "Family-Aware Residual Architecture for Predicting Quantum Circuit
  Simulation Performance", arXiv:2606.11620v1, submitted 2026-06-10.
- Accepted as a full paper at IEEE ISVLSI 2026, QC-CSAA Workshop.
- https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11620

## What The Paper Clearly Covers

Xing et al. target the predictive-routing side of quantum circuit simulation.
Given OpenQASM and an execution context, their model predicts:

- the minimum tensor-network approximation threshold required to reach target
  fidelity `>= 0.75`;
- expected wall-clock runtime for a circuit-threshold pair.

Their architecture treats algorithm family as a first-class feature:

- 32 extracted circuit features;
- family classifier with reported 97.5% accuracy;
- hand-crafted algorithm fingerprints;
- FiLM conditioning and additive family residual heads;
- threshold classification and runtime regression heads.

Reported results:

- 79.5% exact threshold accuracy;
- 91.2% within one threshold rung;
- runtime `R^2 = 0.82`;
- about 50 ms inference latency;
- 7-130 qubits over 10 algorithm families;
- 36 evaluation circuits and 144 execution configurations.

## Claims CAPAS Must Not Make

After this paper, CAPAS should not claim novelty for:

- predicting quantum circuit simulation runtime from circuit structure;
- predicting approximate tensor-network simulation threshold;
- treating algorithm family as a first-class simulation-cost feature;
- using OpenQASM-derived features for simulation cost prediction;
- family-aware simulator parameter selection;
- replacing trial-and-error threshold selection for approximate tensor
  simulation.

Those are now occupied by Xing et al. for the tensor-network simulation setting.

## What Xing et al. Do Not Cover

The paper is a predictor. It is not an evidence trace/profile system.

The paper does not show:

- RO-Crate or PROV export;
- a sealed per-run route/result/evidence artifact;
- `physical_evidence_level`;
- `verification_independence`;
- `reference_truth`;
- `evidenceStatus` for success, none, failed, or rejected;
- separation of solver error, model error, and reference-definition error;
- robust/marginal correctness semantics;
- a physical evidence entity attached to a result.

Their output is operational: choose a threshold and estimate runtime. CAPAS'
output is evidential: record what was decided, what ran, what result was
produced, and what kind of evidence supports that result.

## Complementarity

Xing et al. are best treated as a possible atlas/cost-model component, not as a
CAPAS competitor.

```text
OpenQASM circuit
  -> Xing-style predictor
  -> threshold/runtime prediction
  -> simulator execution
  -> CAPAS run trace
  -> RO-Crate/PROV + PhysicalEvidence
```

Xing et al. answer:

> Which approximate tensor-network threshold is likely sufficient, and how long
> will it run?

CAPAS answers:

> What decision was made, what result was produced, and what evidence supports
> or fails to support that result?

## Limits Of The Xing Result

The paper itself names several limits:

- training set is small: 36 evaluation circuits;
- rare families such as Shor have sparse coverage;
- family-classification errors propagate downstream;
- GPU-specific memory access patterns are not fully captured;
- generalization to other tensor-network simulators is future work;
- uncertainty quantification is future work.

Those limits should not be used to dismiss the paper. They only define where it
could be wrapped, extended, or audited.

## CAPAS Position After This Audit

CAPAS should not compete with Xing et al. on prediction accuracy.

The defensible position is:

> Xing et al. predict simulation thresholds and runtime. CAPAS can package such
> predictions and subsequent executions into sealed RO-Crate/PROV evidence
> traces with explicit physical evidence, witness independence, reference truth,
> and failure/rejection semantics.

## Falsation Condition

Revise the CAPAS gap if Xing et al. or a related public system releases per-run
artifacts that include all of:

- threshold/runtime prediction;
- simulator execution result;
- RO-Crate/PROV packaging;
- explicit physical evidence level;
- witness independence;
- reference truth and numerical error;
- success/no-evidence/failed/rejected evidence states.

If that appears, CAPAS becomes an adapter/profile variant rather than a distinct
evidence layer over simulation-cost predictors.
